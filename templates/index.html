Rules & Info

About Treason

This game is a digital adaptation of the bluffing card game Coup, originally designed by Rikki Tahta and published by Indie Boards & Cards. [web:8][web:11]

Treason Coup is an open‑source web implementation of Coup created by Chris Brown and Jackie Niebling, built on Node.js and inspired this version’s design and flow. [web:2][web:10]

For the best experience, the physical card game is highly recommended if you enjoy the mechanics shown here. [web:8]

Note: This online version currently implements only the core base‑game rules (no Reformation teams or Inquisitor role yet).

Goal & setup

  • Players each start with 2 hidden role cards (“influences”) and 2 coins from the central treasury. [web:8][web:11]
  • The remaining role cards form the Court deck in the middle of the table. [web:8][web:11]
  • The objective is to be the last player with at least one unrevealed influence. [web:8][web:11]

Influence & elimination

  • When you lose an influence, you choose one of your face‑down cards, reveal it, and it no longer provides abilities. [web:8][web:11]
  • When both of your influences are revealed, you are out of the game and your cards stay face up. [web:8][web:11]

Turn flow

  • Play proceeds clockwise; on your turn you must take exactly one action (you cannot pass). [web:8]
  • Other players may then challenge or block, depending on the action. If no one does, the action simply resolves. [web:8][web:10]
  • If you start a turn with 10 or more coins, you are forced to Coup someone that turn. [web:8][web:11]

Always‑available actions

  • Income: Take 1 coin from the treasury. Cannot be blocked or challenged. [web:8][web:10]
  • Foreign Aid: Take 2 coins; can be blocked by someone claiming Duke. [web:8][web:10]
  • Coup (7 coins): Pay 7 coins to force a player to lose an influence; this cannot be blocked or challenged. [web:8][web:11]

Character roles (base game)

  • Duke: Tax – take 3 coins from the treasury; also can block Foreign Aid. [web:8][web:11]
  • Assassin: Assassinate – pay 3 coins to make a target lose an influence, unless they successfully block with Contessa. [web:8][web:11]
  • Captain: Steal – take up to 2 coins from another player; they may block if they claim Captain or Ambassador. [web:8][web:11]
  • Ambassador: Exchange – draw 2 cards from the Court deck, then choose which 2 to keep from your hand plus the drawn cards, returning the rest to the deck. [web:8][web:11]
  • Contessa: Has no action on your turn but can block Assassination against you. [web:8][web:11]

In this implementation you always see your current roles in the lower hand area; the server enforces costs, transfers and influence loss automatically.

Bluffing & challenges

  • You may declare any character action or block, whether or not you actually hold that role. [web:8][web:10]
  • Any other player may challenge your claim; you must then reveal the claimed role if you have it. [web:8][web:11]
  • If you prove the claim, the challenger loses an influence, you shuffle that revealed card back into the deck, and draw a replacement; your original action or block still happens. [web:8][web:11]
  • If you cannot prove the claim, you lose an influence and your action or block is cancelled. [web:8][web:11]

Blocking

  • Some actions can be blocked by specific roles (Duke vs Foreign Aid, Contessa vs Assassinate, Captain/Ambassador vs Steal). [web:8][web:11]
  • Blocks can themselves be challenged exactly like actions; you resolve the challenge first, then either apply or cancel the original action. [web:8][web:10]
  • It is possible to lose two influences in one turn if you both lose a challenge and suffer a successful Assassination against you. [web:8][web:10]

Inquisitor (Reformation)

These rules are shown for players familiar with Coup: Reformation; this role is not yet implemented in this online version. [web:10][web:13][web:16]

  • The Inquisitor replaces the Ambassador in the deck. [web:13][web:16]
  • Exchange one: Draw 1 card from the deck and optionally swap it with one of your influences, then return 1 card to the deck. [web:13][web:16]
  • Examine: Look at one chosen influence of another player, then either let them keep it or force them to draw a new card and put the shown one back in the deck. [web:13][web:16]
  • As a counteraction, the Inquisitor can also block Stealing. [web:13][web:16]

Reformation (teams & treasury)

Reformation adds factions, conversions and a shared treasury reserve; again, this lobby currently uses only base‑game rules. [web:10][web:19]

  • Players belong to one of two factions; many aggressive actions (Coup, Assassinate, Steal, some blocks) cannot be used against teammates. [web:10]
  • Anyone may pay coins to switch their own allegiance or to convert another player to a different side, sending these coins into a separate treasury reserve. [web:10][web:19]
  • Non‑Duke players can attempt to Embezzle, taking all coins from the reserve; if someone successfully proves they actually are a Duke, the embezzler loses influence and returns the money. [web:10][web:19]
  • If eventually everyone ends up on the same team, normal targeting restrictions disappear and play continues until only one player survives. [web:10]
